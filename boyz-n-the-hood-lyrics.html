    Boyz N the Hood, a groundbreaking track released in 1987, remains a defining anthem of gangsta rap and a testament to the raw talent of its creator, Eazy-E.

    As a founding member of N.W.A and a pioneer of West Coast hip-hop, Eazy-E crafted provocative lyrics that vividly depicted the harsh realities of life in South Central Los Angeles.

    With gritty storytelling and unflinching honesty, his verses shed light on the systemic injustices, gang violence, and socioeconomic struggles that plagued his community.

    Facts About Boyz N The Hood Lyrics

    FactDescription
    Song Title“Boyz n the Hood”
    ArtistEazy-E
    Album“N.W.A and the Posse” (1987)
    Songwriter(s)Eazy-E, Ice Cube, D.O.C. (credited as Tracy Curry, O’Shea Jackson, and Andre Young, respectively)
    GenreGangsta rap
    Length5:38
    LabelRuthless Records
    Release Date1987
    Chart PerformanceDid not chart
    Notable FeaturesSampled “I’m a Ho” by Whodini and “The Payback” by James Brown
    LegacyConsidered a seminal track in the development of West Coast gangsta rap, and was later covered by Dynamite Hack
    ControversyThe lyrics contained violent and sexually explicit content, which sparked criticism from some groups
    Influence on Hip-Hop“Boyz n the Hood” is considered one of the most influential gangsta rap songs of all time
    Influence on Pop CultureThe song and its title inspired the 1991 film “Boyz n the Hood,” which was produced by Eazy-E’s record label
