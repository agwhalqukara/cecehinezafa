    Leon Bibb Biography

    Leon Bibb is an American journalist who is currently working as a news anchor and commentator for WKYC in Cleveland, Ohio since joining the station in 2018. Bibb was once a member of the BGSU Board of Trustees and was the first African American primetime news anchor in Ohio.

    Born in Butler, Alabama, Leon was raised in Cleveland’s Glenville neighborhood where he attended Glenville High School. He went on to graduate from the School of Journalism at Bowling Green State University. He also studied Radio-TV-Film in graduate school.

    After graduating, he began his career at NBC television station in 1979 where he worked for 16 years. He then moved to WEWS-TV5, the ABC station in Cleveland where he worked for 22 years. In both stations, he served as a news anchor and reporter for all the major newscasts.

    Throughout his career, he has covered stories throughout Ohio on a regular basis. He has also covered many national stories like the Persian Gulf War. In 2011, he interviewed President Barack Obama in a one-on-one conversation. The conversation took place in the White House. Furthermore, he interviewed President George H.W. Bush.
