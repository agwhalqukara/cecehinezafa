    Breakthrough with “2 Broke Girls”

    Kat Dennings’ career reached new heights with her role in the hit CBS sitcom “2 Broke Girls.” The show, which aired from 2011 to 2017, followed the lives of Max Black (played by Dennings) and Caroline Channing (played by Beth Behrs) as they tried to start a cupcake business while navigating the challenges of being broke. “2 Broke Girls” resonated with audiences and quickly became a fan favorite.

    The success of “2 Broke Girls” can be attributed to its unique blend of humor, relatable characters, and clever writing. The show’s witty dialogue and comedic timing earned it a loyal fanbase and critical acclaim. Dennings’ portrayal of the sassy and sarcastic Max Black endeared her to viewers and showcased her comedic talent.

    One of the factors that contributed to the popularity of “2 Broke Girls” was its syndication royalties. In 2012, the show was sold into syndication for a record-breaking fee of $1.7 million per episode. TBS won the syndication rights and paid a total of $235.6 million for the first run. This meant that Dennings and Behrs not only earned $150,000 per episode but also received equity points, which will result in substantial syndication royalties over the lifetime of the show.

    The success of “2 Broke Girls” catapulted Kat Dennings’ career to new heights and solidified her status as a talented actress in the industry. It opened doors for her to explore a variety of other television and film projects, showcasing her versatility and range as an actress.
