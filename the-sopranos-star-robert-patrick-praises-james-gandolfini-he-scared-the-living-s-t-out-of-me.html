    The Sopranos star Robert Patrick praises James Gandolfini: 'He scared the living s**t out of me'

    With The Sopranos prequel film The Many Saints Of Newark slated to finally premiere in theaters and on HBO Max in October, fans and people involved in the original series have been reminiscing about the impact of the hit crime drama.

    03 Jul 2024, 00:00

    With The Sopranos prequel film – The Many Saints Of Newark – slated to finally premiere in theaters and on HBO Max in October, fans and people involved in the original series have been reminiscing about the impact of the hit crime drama. 

    Former stars Michael Imperioli (Christopher Moltisanti) and Steve Schirripa (Bobby Baccalieri) sat down with Robert Patrick (Davey Scatino) on their podcast Talking Sopranos, where they discussed the greatness of late leading man James Gandolfini (Tony Soprano).   

    During their chat, Patrick, a 62-year-old veteran actor of some 250 films and TV roles, praised Gandolfini, confessing that he was ‘intimidated’ and ‘scared’ after they shot their dramatic scene where Tony goes to collect a poker debt from Davey in the season two episode titled, The Happy Wanderer.

    Revealing: Robert Patrick confessed James Gandolfini ‘intimidated’ and ‘scared’ him after their epic scene together where Tony Soprano beats up high school buddy Davey Scatino

    After talking about what led to him to land the role of Davey Scatino, Patrick mentioned that his acting coach suggested he talk to Gandolfini before filming and tell him to bring his A-game before shooting their epic scene where Tony beats up Davey when he goes to collect the debt. 

    ‘I had never met Mr. Gandolfini,’ the Terminator 2: Judgement Day star said before adding, ‘I am out front on a bench smoking a cigarette with Jimmy [Gandolfini] after we’d just done this read through. And I think we’re trying to get to know each other a little bit because we’re supposed to be high school buddies.’

    He continued, ‘The conversation was winding up, I said to him, “You know the scene where you come to get your money, you better bring your f***ing A-game.” ‘And I looked at him and he looked at me, and he had a cigarette and he said, “Oh, I’ll bring my f***ing A-game.”‘  

    Before walking off, Gandolfini then threw his cigarette at Patrick. 

    Epic: Patrick, a 62-year-old veteran actor of some 250 films and TV roles, appeared on the podcast Talking Sopranos with hosts Steve Schirripa and Michael Imperioli

    Patrick said he had never met Gandolfini before his role on The Sopranos, which began with the episode The Happy Wanderer (season 2, episode 6)

    Patrick said his acting coach suggested he talk to Gandolfini before filming their dramatic and violent scene and tell him to bring his ‘A-game’

    The two actors didn’t see each other again until the day of their scene which begins with Tony barging into Davey’s office of his sporting goods store to collect the $45,000 that he borrowed and lost in the high-stakes poker game.

    ‘He said “How’s your b*lls?” I said, “They’re fine.” And he said, “I’m hungover, let’s do this f***ing scene,”‘ Patrick said. ‘And man, it was one take.’

    He proceeded to give some of the details about how Gandolfini grabbed him, threw his around, and that his character ‘cried like a little girl.’ 

    ‘And I got to tell you, he scared the living s**t out of me. I was so intimidated,’ he admitted with an obvious amount of joy and respect to his face and voice.

    ‘What a fantastic experience.’   

    Tension building: The two actors didn’t see each other until their debt collection scene

    On edge: Patrick said Gandolfini confessed he was hungover just before the director yelled ‘action’ for their confrontational scene

    Monumental: Patrick described how Gandolfini grabbed him, threw his around, and that his character ‘cried like a little girl’

    The three former co-stars went on to describe how Gandolfini had huge hands, which Patrick said, ‘Where coming down on me, and I’m in that chair. What a fantastic experience. I think The Sopranos is definitely one of the highlights of my career.’  

    The Sopranos, which followed the life and crimes of mob boss Tony Soprano, would go on to air for just over six seasons (1999-2007) – 86 episodes in all.

    During the show’s run, Gandolfini won three Emmys for his role as the New Jersey mobster. 

    Sadly, he suffered a heart attack and died in June 2013 at the age of 51.

    His son, Michael Gandolfini, portrays a young version of Tony in the prequel, The Many Saints of Newark, which will hit theaters on October 1 and be on HBO Max for the month of October.
